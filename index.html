First Things First: Your VSF Account

Logging In & Staying Secure

Your key to the digital classroom is your official school account: `yourname@vsf.edu.ph`. Always use this account to access school resources. This section walks you through logging in for the first time and keeping your account safe. For any password issues, remember to see your computer teacher.

Step-by-Step Login
  1. Open your browser and go to classroom.google.com.
  2. Enter your full school email: `yourname@vsf.edu.ph`.
  3. Enter your password.
  4. If asked, choose "I'm A Student".
  5. If you see 2-Step Verification, follow the on-screen instructions.
  6. To switch from a personal account, click your profile icon in the top-right and select your VSF account.
Account Safety 101
  • Create a strong, unique password (letters, numbers, symbols).
  • Never share your password with anyone.
  • Be cautious of "phishing" emails asking for your login details.
  • If you forget your password, do not reset it yourself. Ask your computer teacher for help.

Use Chat for quick questions and group projects. It's great for collaborating on Docs, Sheets, and Slides in real-time.

Chat Etiquette & Safety

  • Think before you type; be clear and respectful.
  • Keep conversations professional and on-topic.
  • Never share personal information like your address or phone number.
  • Report any form of cyberbullying to a teacher immediately.

Meet is for your online classes and discussions. You can join from Classroom, a link, or a nickname. Remember, only users with a school account can join VSF meetings, ensuring a secure environment.

How to Participate

  • Toggle your camera and microphone on/off.
  • Use the "Hand Raise" feature to ask to speak.

Being a Responsible Digital Citizen

Digital citizenship is about being safe, respectful, and responsible online. It's about treating others well and protecting yourself in the digital world. Your VSF account is a private, ad-free space to help you practice these skills.

1. Empathy & Respect (Netiquette)

Remember there's a real person behind every screen. Treat others online as you would in person. Be clear and compassionate in your messages, and never engage in or tolerate cyberbullying.

2. Privacy & Security

Protect your personal information. Use strong passwords and be wary of phishing scams. Understand that the school administrator can access your account activity to ensure safety and compliance.

3. Digital Literacy & Smart Use

Think critically about the information you find online. Check sources and be skeptical of claims that seem too good to be true. Everything you post creates a "digital footprint" that can be permanent.

4. Healthy Balance

Technology is a tool. It's important to balance your time online with offline activities, real-world interactions, and physical exercise.
